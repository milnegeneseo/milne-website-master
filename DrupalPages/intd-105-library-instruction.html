Basic library session objectives

Goal I: Select resources appropriate for assignments by being able to differentiate between scholarly, popular and trade materials.

Students will:

  1. Identify characteristics of a popular, scholarly or trade publication (online or in print).
  2. Define "peer-reviewed" as a subset within scholarly resources (and recognize that some scholarly journals are not peer-reviewed).
  3. Select a scholarly journal article from a group of articles.

 

Goal II: Access an assortment of resources available in various formats and locations.

Students will:

  1. Identify the format of a resource from a results list (such as print books, e-books, online articles, print articles, book reviews, websites, multimedia, etc.)
  2. Demonstrate an understanding of the physical location of print and multimedia resources in Milne Library.
  3. Demonstrate an understanding of how to access materials electronically through databases, and other online mediums.
  4. Understand how and determine when to request items through IDS.

 

Goal III: Promote the use of appropriate resources and services to satisfy student research needs.

Students will:

  1. Determine appropriateness of using multidisciplinary and/or subject-specific databases.
  2. Identify recommended databases, reference sources, web sites, etc. pertaining to a specific subject (e.g. through Resources by Subject pages, myCourses, etc.).
  3. Understand what content can be found in WorldCat Local.
  4. Have an awareness of available help (self-service and mediated) - i.e. writing, citing, research, ask a librarian, consultations, IM, library web site, etc.

Please direct comments and questions to Bonnie Swoger, swoger@geneseo.edu.


Making the most out of a library session

  • Library sessions should be scheduled close to the time when students begin their research-based projects.
  • To maximize a library session, students should have a clear understanding of their assigned project and have a good idea of a potential topic. 
  • Consider developing research-based assignments in consultation with a librarian.
  • Students focus more fully in a library session when the lesson relates to an upcoming research-based assignment.
  • The chosen learning objectives above represent a realistic amount of content that can be covered in a one-time 50-minute session.  If professors desire training that goes beyond these objectives, a second library session is highly recommended.
  • Past research indicates that students score higher on library skills assessment if they attend more than one session of library instruction.
