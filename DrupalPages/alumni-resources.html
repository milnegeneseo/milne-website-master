Courtesy Borrowing Library Card

SUNY Geneseo alumni have borrowing privileges to check out books from Milne Library's circulating collection at no charge. To apply for a courtesy borrowing library cards, please visit the Service desk for more information about your borrowing privileges and how to obtain a courtesy card.

Library Databases

You may search for articles in online journals, reference tools, newspapers, etc. from any Milne library public-access computers; to use these computers, please see Circulation. Unfortunately the terms of licensing most subscription databases, i.e. JSTOR, Science Direct, etc. limit off-campus access to current students, staff or faculty.

Databases freely available on the Internet:

  • Agricola - Citations to the agricultural literature created by the National Agricultural Library and its cooperators.
  • ArXiv - Open access to e-prints in Physics, Math, Computer Science, Quantitative Biology, Quantitative Finance and Statistics.
  • CIA World Factbook - Information on the history, people, government, economy, etc... for world entities.
  • ERIC - ERIC The world's largest digital library of education literature.
  • GPO Access - Official, full-text information from the three branches of the Federal Government.
  • PubMed - Provides access to the 9 million citations in MEDLINE and Pre-MEDLINE and other related databases.
  • Worldcat.org - Connects you to the collections and services of more than 10,000 libraries worldwide.
  • Library of Congress Virtual Reference Shelf - Online reference materials in all subjects.
  • Google Scholar - Search engine for scholarly information from institutional repositories, publisher websites, etc...
  • Scirus - Search engine concentrated on scholarly sources in the sciences, including publisher websites.

Other Library Databases

Some New York Public Library databases can be remotely accessed once you obtain a NYPL Library card - eligibility is limited to people who live and/or work in New York State. See the New York Public Library for details:


New York State Library provides free access to databases through NOVELny - New York Online Virtual Electronic Library. Using a New York State Driver's License, residents can remotely access resources such as Business & Company Resource Center, ProQuest Platinum, Grolier Online (includes Encyclopedia Americana), Health & Wellness Resource Center and more.
